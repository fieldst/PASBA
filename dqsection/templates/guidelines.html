End of Day Processing

Daily EOD processing is important as it reconciles all appointments with the actual workload of the clinic. All clinics must perform EOD processing daily to close out all pending appointments and ensure workload is transmitted to appropriate data repositories and other applications. View the End of Day Processing Guide

Beneficiary Insurance Information

Inclusion of the DD Form 2569 in non-active duty medical records is an audit item established in the FY04 DQMC Program. View the Memorandum, MEDCOM, 26 March 2003, Subject: Requirement to Identify, Document, and Update Beneficiary Insurance Information

ADM Compliance Report

The ADM Compliance Report is a tool to utilize and identify how many outpatient visits have not been coded within three business days following the appointment. View the ADM Compliance Report Instructions. This document contains step-by-step instructions and CHCS screen shots for running the ADM Complaince Reprot by clinic.

Tracking Ambulatory Procedure Visits

The CHCS menu option for tracking APVs works similarly to the ADM Outpatient Visits Report Option in ADM. It is a valuable tool to assist a data quality manager in tracking the timely completion of ambulatory procedures visits within 15 days of the encounter. View the instructions for Tracking Ambulatory Procedure Visits in CHCS
