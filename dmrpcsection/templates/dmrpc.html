Deployed Medical Records Processing Center

Mission

Provides expertise in processing electronic patient records and workload data received from US Army Medical Treatment Facilities (MTFs) for input into the AMEDD central data repository by PASBA. Supports and process Inpatient and Outpatient Medical Records generated at all theaters and contingency operation locations.

DMRPC service includes:

  • Diagnosis and procedure coding (inpatient only)
  • Data entry into the AMEDD central repository
  • Record scanning
  • Inpatient record retirement to the National Personnel Record Center in Rock Island, IL
  • Inpatient Detainee record retirement to the Defense Logistics Agency Document Service, OIF (Pensacola, FL) and OEF (Kings Bay, GA)
  • Outpatient records retirement to the AMEDD Record Processing Center in Fort Sam Houston, TX
  • The Data Input Branch serves as the functional manager for the Army SIDR transmittals, receiving monthly SIDR transmissions and assisting the Army MTFs with errors and discrepancies

The Deployed Medical Records Processing Center (DMPRC) provides expertise in the following areas:

  • Processing electronic patient records and workload data received from the US ARMY medical treatment facilities (MTF’S) for input into the AMEDD central data repository and the MHS Data Repository (MDR).
  • Supports the Deployed MTF’s by receiving and processing inpatient treatment records and retiring the completed records to the National Personnel Records Center (NPRC).
  • Supports the Deployed MTF’S by receiving and processing miscellaneous outpatient medical documentation and preparing the documentation for final disposition to the AMEDD Records Processing Center (ARPC).
  • In addition to providing guidance; the DMRPC is the primary point of contact for managing medical documentation for military, federal civilian employees and other personnel eligible for care in treatment facilities during contingency operations.
  • The DMRPC provides data for multiple PASBA applications and reporting systems that are utilized AMEDD wide.
