End of Day Processing

Daily EOD processing is important as it reconciles all appointments with the actual workload of the clinic. All clinics must perform EOD processing daily to close out all pending appointments and ensure workload is transmitted to appropriate data repositories and other applications. View the End of Day Processing Guide, May 2002

Beneficiary Insurance Information

Inclusion of the DD Form 2569 in non-active duty medical records is a new audit item for the FY04 DQMC Program. View the Memorandum, MEDCOM, 26 March 2003, Subject: Requirement to Identify, Document, and Update Beneficiary Insurance Information

ADM Compliance Report

The ADM Compliance Report is used to determine how many outpatient visits have not been coded and submitted as Standard Ambulatory Data Record to PASBA within three business days following the appointment. The result of this report must be added to the denominator of the data provided separately by PASBA to determine your MTF's compliance rate. View the ADM Compliance Report Instructions December 2003. Tracking Ambulatory Patient Visits

The CHCS menu option for tracking APVs works similarly to the ADM Outpatient Visits Report Option in ADM. It is a valuable tool to assist a data quality manager in tracking the timely completion of ambulatory procedures visits within 15 days of the encounter. View the instructions for Tracking Ambulatory Patient Visits in CHCS, Mar 2004
